# Magnus the Mercedes Driver - Tertiary Persona

> TERTIARY target - The premium buyer who justifies V2 supplier integration and drives margin

**Priority:** TERTIARY (V2)
**Role in Flywheel:** Magnus's demand for premium brands drives Sharif to integrate local supplier catalogs, which expands the product range for everyone.
**Created:** 2026-03-26

---

## Profile Summary

**Magnus drives a Mercedes E-Class and wants Pirelli or Continental on his car. He knows exactly which brand and model he wants. He's willing to pay for quality but still shops around for the best deal. Sharif's budget Chinese tires aren't for him — but Sharif's supplier network could source his Pirellis at a better price than the dealer.**

Magnus represents the premium segment that V1 deliberately excludes. Serving him requires on-demand ordering from local suppliers (Starco, etc.), longer fulfillment times, and a larger catalog. He's the V2 expansion opportunity.

## Background

### Daily Life

**Work:** Business owner or senior professional. Success is visible in his car choice.

**Car Relationship:** His car is an extension of his identity. He maintains it properly. Tires are not a chore — they're a quality decision. He has preferences and opinions.

**Decision Style:** Brand-first. He decides on Pirelli P Zero or Continental SportContact, then shops for the best price on that specific product. He's not browsing — he's sourcing.

---

## Driving Forces

### Top 3 Positive Drivers (What He Wants)

**1. His Specific Brand and Model — No substitutes**
- He wants Pirelli P Zero 245/40R19, not "a tire that fits"
- Brand trust is non-negotiable — these tires are tested for his car
- Success: search his exact tire, find it available, competitive price
- **Sharif Promise (V2):** On-demand sourcing from local suppliers. If Starco has it, Sharif can get it.

**2. Competitive Price on Premium — Not cheap, but fair**
- He knows what Pirellis cost at the dealer. He's looking for 10-20% less.
- He'll order from Sweden if the price is right
- Success: same tire, better price than the Mercedes dealer or Dekkmann
- **Sharif Promise (V2):** Direct supplier relationships cut out middlemen. Price transparency.

**3. Professional Service — Mounting by people who know luxury cars**
- His car has TPMS sensors, staggered wheel setups, torque specs
- He doesn't trust a budget shop with his Mercedes
- Success: booking at a workshop that handles premium vehicles
- **Sharif Promise (V2):** Partner workshop network includes quality-certified stations.

---

### Top 3 Negative Drivers (What He Fears)

**1. Damage to His Vehicle**
- Wrong mounting, scratched rims, incorrect torque
- His car is worth 800K NOK — the tires must be handled properly
- **Sharif Answer (V2):** Premium workshop partners, explicit care guarantees.

**2. Counterfeit or Grey Market Tires**
- He wants authorized, DOT-current tires
- Concerned about buying "cheap" and getting old stock
- **Sharif Answer (V2):** Sourced from authorized Norwegian distributors, DOT dates visible.

**3. Settling for Second Best**
- He won't put budget tires on a Mercedes. Period.
- If Sharif only shows Powertrac, he leaves immediately.
- **Sharif Answer (V2):** Brand filter shows premium brands. If not in stock, "Available in 3-5 days from our suppliers."

## How Magnus Discovers Sharif Value

### The Recognition Path

**Phase 1: Not Yet (V1)**
- Magnus searches "Pirelli P Zero 245/40R19 pris Norge"
- Sharif doesn't appear — no premium brands in V1 catalog
- He buys from Bythjul or the dealer

**Phase 2: V2 Discovery**
- Sharif adds supplier integration. His Pirellis appear on the site.
- He lands via SEO ("Pirelli P Zero 245/40R19 billig") or brand page
- Enters the same smooth flow, but with his premium product
- Price is 15% less than the dealer

**Phase 3: Appreciation**
- Same great experience as Harriet and Ole, but with his product
- Books mounting at a quality partner workshop
- Gets his car back with proper torque and no scratches

**Phase 4: Word of Mouth**
- Tells his car club / Mercedes forum
- "There's this site that actually has Pirellis at a good price AND proper mounting"
- Drives high-value, high-margin referrals

## Impact on Business Goals

**Magnus's Role in Success Metrics:**

**V2 Revenue:**
- Premium tires = higher margin per order
- His brand demands push Sharif to build supplier integrations that benefit everyone
- His word-of-mouth reaches high-value customers

**Catalog Expansion:**
- His needs justify the investment in supplier API integration
- Once built for Magnus, the same infrastructure serves Ole's brand preferences
- The full catalog (budget + premium) makes Sharif a one-stop destination

---

## Why V2, Not V1

Magnus requires:
- On-demand ordering from local suppliers (Starco, etc.)
- Longer fulfillment (3-5 day delivery vs. in-stock)
- Premium workshop partnerships (not all shops handle luxury cars)
- Larger catalog management (thousands of SKUs vs. ~500)

These are all V2 features. Including Magnus in V1 would complicate the flow, confuse Harriet, and delay launch. **His absence in V1 is a feature, not a limitation.**
