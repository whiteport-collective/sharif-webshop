# Harriet the Hairdresser - Primary Persona

> PRIMARY target - The customer who proves the model and drives word-of-mouth

**Priority:** PRIMARY
**Role in Flywheel:** Harriet is the proof. If she can buy tires in under 3 minutes without confusion, anyone can. Every satisfied Harriet tells her friends.
**Created:** 2026-03-26

---

## Profile Summary

**Harriet is an independent woman in her 30s who drives a small car from home to her salon. She does everything herself — even if she has a partner, she doesn't let anyone do things for her. She doesn't care about cars or tires. They should just work. When they don't, she handles it without complaining — but she always takes the path of least resistance.**

She represents the largest untapped segment of the Norwegian tire market: people who currently default to their local tire shop and pay whatever it costs because shopping around is too confusing and time-consuming. If Sharif can capture Harriet, they capture the mass market.

## Background

### Daily Life

**Work:** Runs or works at a hair salon. Socially active, digitally native, manages bookings and social media for the salon.

**Tech Comfort:** High — she's on Instagram, TikTok, books everything on her phone, uses Vipps, orders food delivery. Not intimidated by digital experiences, but has zero patience for bad ones.

**Car Relationship:** Purely functional. Drives a small hatchback. Gets fuel when the light comes on. Brings it to EU kontroll when reminded. Tires are something she deals with when she absolutely has to.

**Decision Style:** Fast. She doesn't research. She doesn't compare. She picks the path of least resistance and moves on. If something takes more than a few minutes, she'll either abandon it or call someone.

---

## Current Situation

### The Tire Reality

**The Daily Struggle:**
- She notices her tires look worn but has no idea what to do about it
- She doesn't know her tire dimensions and has never looked at the side of a tire
- She asks a colleague or googles "bytte dekk" and gets overwhelmed by technical specs
- She ends up calling or walking into the nearest local shop and paying whatever they charge

**Current Solution:**
- Goes to whoever is closest and most convenient
- Pays full price without comparing
- Gets it done but feels like she probably overpaid
- Doesn't think about tires again until next time

**The Gap:**
- Every tire site is designed for people who know about tires
- The language is technical, the choices are overwhelming
- There's no "just give me tires that work for my car at a good price" option
- She'd love to just tap a few things on her phone and be done — like booking a haircut

## Psychological Profile

### Personality & Motivations

**Core Identity:**
- Independent — does everything herself, asks no one for help
- Efficient — values her time above almost everything
- Pragmatic — not interested in the "best" tire, just one that works
- Social — trusts recommendations from friends over brands

**Decision Style:**
- Gut feeling + price. If it looks trustworthy and the price is right, she's in.
- Won't read reviews, compare specs, or scroll through options
- Defaults to the easiest path — if your flow is harder than calling a shop, you've lost her

**Communication Preferences:**
- Visual over text
- Short, clear, no jargon
- Feels patronized by "educational" content — don't teach her about tires, just help her buy them

---

## Driving Forces

### Top 3 Positive Drivers (What She Wants)

**1. Speed — Get it done in minutes, not hours**
- She wants to go from "I need tires" to "sorted" in the time it takes to scroll Instagram
- Her time is precious and tires don't deserve much of it
- Success: tires ordered and mounting booked in under 3 minutes
- **Sharif Promise:** The continuous guided flow — 6 steps, one surface, done

**2. Simplicity — No decisions that require tire knowledge**
- She doesn't know what rolling resistance means and doesn't want to learn
- Every technical choice is a moment she might abandon the purchase
- Success: she picks quantity and location, the system handles the rest
- **Sharif Promise:** Dimension input with visual help, products sorted by price, EU labels translated to plain language

**3. Fair Price — Confidence she's not being ripped off**
- She suspects the local shop overcharges but has no way to compare
- She doesn't need the cheapest — she needs to feel the price is fair
- Success: clear pricing, no hidden fees, no upselling
- **Sharif Promise:** Lowest prices from direct import, transparent pricing, no tricks

---

### Top 3 Negative Drivers (What She Fears)

**1. Looking Stupid — Being exposed as someone who knows nothing about tires**
- She's competent in her world (salon, social media, life) but tires are alien territory
- Technical jargon makes her feel dumb and she'll leave immediately
- Failure: a site that asks her questions she can't answer
- **Sharif Answer:** The flow never assumes knowledge. Visual help for finding dimensions. Plain language everywhere. No jargon.

**2. Making the Wrong Choice — Buying tires that don't fit or aren't safe**
- She has no way to validate if she's picking the right product
- Fear of getting it wrong is worse than overpaying
- Failure: returns, wrong size, unsafe tires
- **Sharif Answer:** Dimension input ensures correct size. Products are pre-filtered — you can't pick wrong. The system only shows what fits.

**3. Wasting Time — Starting a process that turns into a rabbit hole**
- She's been burned by complex checkout flows before (abandoned carts, endless forms)
- If step 2 looks harder than step 1, she's gone
- Failure: a flow that gets progressively more complex or asks for information she doesn't have
- **Sharif Answer:** Each step is simpler than the last. The parallax flow feels like it's pulling you forward, not pushing you through hoops. Booking is after payment — by then she's committed.

## What Harriet Needs to See on Sharif.no

**1. The Dimension Input**
- Visual guide showing exactly where to find the numbers on her tire
- Three simple dropdowns — nothing else on screen
- Feels like the start of something, not the middle of something

**2. Products**
- Sorted by price (cheapest first — that's her default)
- Each tire: image, price, plain-language description ("Solid everyday tire, good in rain")
- Visual sliders for EU labels — not letters and numbers
- No overwhelming grid of 50 options — show the best few

**3. Quantity**
- Simple 2 or 4 toggle
- Total price updates immediately

**4. Shop Selection**
- Map or simple list with distance
- "Fjellhamar" or "Drammen" — two taps

**5. Payment**
- Big satisfying "Betal na" button
- Klarna/Qliro handles the rest
- She never has to enter tire information again

**6. Booking**
- "Done! Pick a time to get them mounted."
- Calendar with available slots
- Confirmation: date, time, shop, what she bought
