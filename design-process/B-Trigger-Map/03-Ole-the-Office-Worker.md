# Ole the Office Worker - Secondary Persona

> SECONDARY target - The informed buyer who validates catalog quality and drives higher-value orders

**Priority:** SECONDARY
**Role in Flywheel:** Ole validates that Sharif isn't just cheap — it's smart. His presence signals quality. His brand filtering and EV needs push the catalog to mature.
**Created:** 2026-03-26

---

## Profile Summary

**Ole is a 40-something office worker who drives a Tesla Model 3. He knows about cars, pays attention to tire tests, and may have a brand preference. He's not chasing the cheapest option — he wants the right tire at a fair price. He compares options but doesn't waste time on bad sites.**

Ole represents the growing EV segment in Norway (~80% of new car sales). His needs are specific: narrower tires, higher load ratings, low noise, EV-optimized models. Most Norwegian tire sites ignore this completely — a massive content gap.

## Background

### Daily Life

**Work:** Office-based professional. Commutes in his Tesla. Technically literate, reads Tek.no and Motor.no, follows EV news.

**Tech Comfort:** Very high. Uses comparison tools, reads reviews, trusts data over marketing.

**Car Relationship:** Engaged. Chose his Tesla deliberately. Understands range, charging, and how tires affect both. Has opinions about brands.

**Decision Style:** Research-then-decide. Spends 15-30 minutes comparing options, reads 2-3 reviews, then commits. Won't overpay but won't buy the cheapest if the quality difference is real.

---

## Current Situation

### The Tire Reality

**The Daily Struggle:**
- Knows his Tesla needs specific tires (load rating, noise, rolling resistance)
- Most tire sites don't filter for EV needs
- Has to manually check load ratings and noise levels across products
- Frustrated by sites that treat all cars the same

**Current Solution:**
- Searches "dekk til Tesla Model 3" and reads forums
- Checks Bythjul or Dekkmann, manually filters for his needs
- Sometimes orders from Sweden for better prices
- Gets tires mounted at a local workshop separately

**The Gap:**
- No Norwegian tire site understands EV-specific needs
- He'd love a site that automatically surfaces EV-friendly options based on his dimensions
- Willing to pay a small premium for a smarter experience

## Driving Forces

### Top 3 Positive Drivers (What He Wants)

**1. Right Tire for His EV — Specs that actually match his Tesla's needs**
- Higher load rating (EVs are heavy), low rolling resistance (range), low noise (no engine to mask it)
- Wants to see these specs clearly, not hunt for them in fine print
- Success: enters his dimensions, sees EV-optimized options highlighted
- **Sharif Promise:** Dimensions automatically identify likely EV sizes. EU label sliders show noise and rolling resistance prominently. EV-friendly badges on suitable tires.

**2. Quality at a Fair Price — Not cheapest, but best value**
- Willing to pay more for a tire that's quieter and lasts longer
- Wants to see the quality difference between options clearly
- Success: easy comparison between budget and mid-range options with clear trade-offs
- **Sharif Promise:** EU label sliders make comparison instant. AI-generated descriptions explain trade-offs in plain language. Brand filter for those with preferences.

**3. Efficiency — Don't waste his time with a clunky site**
- He's willing to spend 10-15 minutes (not Harriet's 2 minutes) but won't tolerate bad UX
- Expects a modern, responsive experience
- Success: smooth flow, clear information, no page reloads
- **Sharif Promise:** The continuous parallax flow is modern and fast. Filters expand for those who want them. Information layers reveal on demand.

---

### Top 3 Negative Drivers (What He Fears)

**1. Wrong Tire Compromises Range or Safety**
- A tire with wrong load rating could be dangerous on a heavy EV
- High rolling resistance kills range — he tracks this
- Failure: buying a "car tire" that's not rated for EV weight
- **Sharif Answer:** Product data includes load ratings. EV-aware filtering catches mismatches before purchase.

**2. Overpaying Due to Poor Information**
- He knows prices vary 30-50% between sources for the same tire
- Hates the feeling of finding a better deal after purchase
- Failure: paying Dekkmann prices when Sharif has it cheaper
- **Sharif Answer:** Transparent pricing from direct import. No games, no fake discounts, just consistently low prices.

**3. Settling for a Generic Experience**
- He's used to Tesla's UX — sleek, smart, responsive
- A clunky tire site feels like going backwards
- Failure: a WordPress-looking store with tiny images and pagination
- **Sharif Answer:** The continuous parallax flow feels premium. Modern, animated, responsive. The experience matches his expectation from premium digital products.

## Validation Strategy

### What Ole Needs to See About Sharif.no

**1. Technical Credibility**
- EU labels displayed properly and explained
- Load ratings visible
- EV-relevant specs highlighted
- Proof that the site understands tire technology

**2. Brand Availability**
- Can he filter by brand? (Yes — collapsed filter, available when needed)
- Are there recognizable mid-range brands? (Depends on Moohsen's stock)
- If not, are the budget brands presented with enough data to make an informed choice?

**3. Price Advantage**
- Clear, competitive pricing
- Obvious savings vs. chain stores
- No hidden fees (mounting, disposal, etc.)

**4. Modern Experience**
- The site itself is the proof — if it's this smooth, the company is serious
- Mobile experience that matches what he expects from modern apps

## Conversion Path

### How Ole Validates Sharif.no

**Phase 1: Discovery**
- Searches "dekk til Tesla Model 3" or "billige vinterdekk 235/45R18"
- Lands on a car model SEO page or dimension page
- First impression: "This looks different from other tire sites"

**Phase 2: Evaluation**
- Enters his dimensions, sees products with EU label sliders
- Checks load ratings, noise levels — the data is all there
- Compares prices to what he'd pay elsewhere
- Maybe uses the brand filter to check options

**Phase 3: Purchase**
- Selects tires, picks quantity, chooses shop
- Smooth flow confirms this is a legitimate operation
- Pays via Klarna/Qliro

**Phase 4: Advocacy**
- Shares with his Tesla owners group or EV forum
- "Found this Norwegian site that actually understands EV tires"
- Drives high-value referrals from the EV community
