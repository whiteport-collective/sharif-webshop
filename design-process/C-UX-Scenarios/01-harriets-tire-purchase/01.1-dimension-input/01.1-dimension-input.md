# 01.1 — Dimension Input

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|

| **Platform** | Mobile (iPhone, portrait) |

## Overview

**Page Purpose:** Harriet enters her tire dimensions — the first and only barrier to entry. This view must make a tire-ignorant person feel confident enough to type three numbers.

**Entry Context:** Harriet tapped a geo-targeted Instagram ad ("Sommerdekk fra 499kr — montering inkludert"). This is her first impression of Sharif.no.

**User Situation:** Between clients at the salon, 5 minutes to spare, phone in hand. She's never looked at a tire sidewall before.

**Mental State:**
- **Hope:** Get this sorted fast, find cheap tires
- **Worry:** Will I understand what they're asking me?

**Discovery Method:** Instagram ad → taps through → lands here

**Exit Action:** Enters dimension (or pastes from a photo), taps "Finn dekk" → products appear below via parallax transition

## On-Page Interactions

- **Tire guide toggle** — taps "Hvor finner jeg dette?" to see visual guide (celebrity UGC content showing tire sidewall markings). Opens as overlay, not navigation.
- **Hybrid input** — can type/paste "205/55R16" directly, or use three dropdown selectors (width/profile/rim) that sync with the text field as she types
- **Smart parsing** — if she pastes "205/55 R16" or "205 55 16" from a photo, the system parses it and fills the dropdowns

## Design Notes

- This is the TOP of the continuous parallax surface — the beginning of the flow
- Background: subtle tire texture or racing stripe pattern (retro heritage)
- The input area should feel like the only thing on screen — focused, not cluttered
- The illustration should make finding the dimension feel obvious, not educational
- Red CTA button — high contrast against the dark/neutral background
- Trust bar anchors the bottom — three compact value propositions
