# 01.2 — Product Cards

## Page Metadata

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Scenario** | 01: Harriet's Tire Purchase |
| **Page Number** | 01.2 |
| **Platform** | Mobile (iPhone, portrait) |

## Overview

**Page Purpose:** Harriet sees her matching tires and picks one. Products are presented as horizontally swiping cards sorted by price — cheapest first. This is where she makes her decision.

**Entry Context:** She entered her dimension (e.g., 185/65R15) and tapped "Finn dekk". The parallax surface scrolls/transitions down to reveal the product cards beneath the dimension input.

**Exit Action:** Taps a product card → product detail overlay opens (view 01.3)

## On-Page Interactions

- **Horizontal swipe** — cards swipe left/right. First card (cheapest) is pre-selected/highlighted
- **Filter toggle** — small "Filter" button reveals collapsed filter overlay (brand, season). Harriet ignores it. Ole uses it.
- **Price tag prominent** — each card leads with price per tire
- **Stock badge** — green "På lager" / amber "Kun 4 igjen" / grey "Utsolgt"
- **Card tap** — opens product detail as overlay (01.3), not navigation

## Content Elements

| Element | Type | Content |

## Design Notes

- Cards should feel like physical objects you're flipping through — depth, shadow, slight tilt on swipe
- The cheapest card is the default/hero position — Harriet sees it first
- EU label sliders are tiny visual bars, not text — color-coded green to red
- Price is the largest text on each card — it's Harriet's primary decision factor
- The parallax transition from dimension input to cards should feel like looking "deeper" into the surface
- Background darkens slightly as we move deeper into the flow
