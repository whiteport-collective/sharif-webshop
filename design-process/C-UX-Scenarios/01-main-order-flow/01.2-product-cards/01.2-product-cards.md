## Overview

**Page Purpose:** Present matching tires as browsable cards so Harriet can compare options and pick one.

**User Situation:** Harriet just entered her tire dimension (205/55R16) and tapped "Finn dekk". The parallax surface transitions down to reveal product cards. She has 5 minutes between clients, phone in hand, and wants to quickly find an affordable tire.

**Success Criteria:** User identifies a tire that fits her needs and taps a card to see details.

**Entry Points:**
- Parallax transition from 01.1-Dimension Input after submitting a valid dimension
- Direct URL with dimension parameter (shared link, bookmark)

**Exit Points:**
- Taps **Velg disse** on card CTA → add to cart → **01.4-Delivery and Mounting** (primary checkout path)
- Taps a product card (image/name area) → **01.3-Product Detail** (browse/compare path)
- Taps "Edit" on dimension bar → scrolls back to 01.1-Dimension Input
- Taps filter button → filter overlay opens (brand, season)

## Layout Structure

Horizontal card carousel below a sticky dimension summary bar. Part of the continuous parallax surface — not a separate page load.

**Responsive Behavior:**
- **Mobile (< 768px):** Single card visible with partial peek of next card. Horizontal swipe. Dimension bar sticky below header.
- **Tablet (768px - 1024px):** Two cards visible side by side, swipeable. Wider card padding.
- **Desktop (>= 1024px):** Grid layout (3-4 cards per row), no swipe — standard scroll. Filter sidebar instead of collapsed overlay.

### Step-by-step

**Step 1 — Default state**

The card CTA shows a split button: `[4 st ▾]` on the left (quantity picker) and `[Velg disse]` on the right. Harriet selects her quantity using the picker (default: 4), then taps **Velg disse**.

**Step 2 — Add to cart + navigate**

Tapping **Velg disse** does two things simultaneously:
1. Adds the selected tire (in the chosen quantity) to the shopping cart
2. Opens **01.4 Delivery and Mounting** — where Harriet picks a mounting shop, then pays

This is the primary checkout path. No intermediate confirmation screen.

**Step 3 — Back: in-cart state**

If Harriet taps **← Tilbake** from 01.4 (e.g. changed her mind), she returns to the results view. The card she selected now shows an in-cart button instead of **Velg disse**:

`[✓ 4 st i handlekurven]` `[🗑]`

The green area and the trash icon are separate tap targets:

| Tap | Behavior |
|-----|----------|
| Green area `✓ 4 st i handlekurven` | Reopens 01.4 Delivery and Mounting for this product |
| Trash `🗑` | Removes product from cart — button reverts to default `[4 st ▾ \| Velg disse]` |

This makes the "oops" recovery obvious without a modal or confirmation dialog — Harriet sees exactly what's in her cart and can undo with one tap.

## Technical Notes

- Part of the continuous parallax surface — no page load between 01.1 and 01.2
- Cards sorted by price ascending (cheapest first) by default
- Horizontal scroll uses CSS scroll-snap-type: x mandatory for card snapping
- Card peek: ~20% of next card visible to hint horizontal scrolling
- Dimension bar is sticky (position: sticky) below the global header
- EU label mini-sliders are purely visual — no interaction, decorative ARIA role
- Desktop breakpoint switches from carousel to grid layout (no horizontal scroll)
- **Browser back:** Tapping browser back from this view returns to 01.1-Dimension Input via `popstate` — the panel animates back, no page reload. The URL reverts to `/no` (clean, no query string).
- **Back button in UI:** The "← Back" button in the results header calls `handleBack()`, which also cleans the URL to `/no` via `pushState`.

### Instant results with skeleton loading

The product count per dimension is precomputed on the server when building the dimension selector. On submit:

1. The results panel transitions in **immediately** — no waiting for data
2. The known count (e.g. "3 dekk funnet") is shown at once from the precomputed map
3. Skeleton cards (shimmer placeholders) fill the carousel matching the known count
4. The actual product fetch runs in the background via a server action
5. When data arrives, skeletons are replaced by real product cards in place

This means the panel transition and count are always instant — the user sees the correct number of cards animating in before the product images and details load.
